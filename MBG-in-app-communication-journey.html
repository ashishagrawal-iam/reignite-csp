MBG — the CSP's in-app journey

A stage-by-stage communication plan for the Minimum Business Guarantee inside the Wiom CSP app — from first hearing about the scheme, to opting in, to seeing eligibility update day-to-day, to being warned when at-risk, to the moment a parameter fails, through to payout and the September sunset. Each stage names the trigger, the app surface it uses (built on existing components — banner, assurance chip, bottom sheet, toast, dialog), the Hindi-first copy, and a screen mockup rendered in the real Wiom design tokens.

One-sentence rule (BV4): the CSP must understand MBG in a single line Sunset framing: "3-month volume bridge", never "Wiom is investing in you" — avoids the Ola-2017 backlash No-blame (Wiom way): a miss is reframed as next-month opportunity; volume shortfalls are owned by Wiom, not the CSP Show the cliff with eyes open: the 70% gate is never hidden — it is turned into a "one more install" nudge Hindi-first: all copy in Noto Sans Devanagari, warm register Server-driven: banners, chips & sheets carry no hardcoded state — same render pipeline the app already uses

The journey mirrors the Cold Start staged solution: Stages 1–9 are the Igniter (Compact + Guarantee); Stage 10 is the controlled hand-off to the Earned-Flow Ladder (the persistence rail).

Stage 1 · Awareness

The CSP hears about MBG

A signed-and-shortlisted CSP needs to learn the scheme exists and grasp it in one line — before any sign-up friction.

Trigger: scheme launch / CSP shortlisted Push (CleverTap) → Home banner → Info sheet Tone: opportunity

Three touchpoints, escalating in detail. The push pulls them in; the home banner persists until acknowledged; the info sheet does the actual explaining — and stops at the one-sentence rule before offering "learn the terms".

  • Push notification on the lock screen — single benefit hook, deep-links to Home (CleverTap in-apps are Home-allowlisted, so the banner is waiting when they land).
  • Home info banner (purple INFO severity) sits above the assurance strip; tapping opens the sheet. Dismissible, but reappears until opt-in.
  • Info bottom sheet leads with the one-sentence definition (BV4), then the two-way promise, then the hard 3-month window so temporariness is felt from word one.
Why this framing: the sheet says "यह मौका सिर्फ़ 3 महीने का है — अभी क्षमता बनाएँ" (a 3-month window — build capacity now), not "Wiom is investing in your business." Same content, but the first makes the September sunset feel built-in and sidesteps the Ola-2017 entitlement trap flagged in the Cold Start analysis.

Stage 2 · Opt-in (the Mutual Compact)

Both sides commit

Opt-in is the Mutual Compact. The sheet shows what Wiom commits and what the CSP commits, side by side — the contractual rail that makes behaviour persist.

Trigger: taps "जानें / शामिल हों" Compact bottom sheet → confirm → toast Tone: mutual, plain
  • Two-way commitment, visible. The sheet renders Wiom's promise (weekly leads) and the CSP's promise (70% install rate) as paired rows — the compact's "neither side moves first" deadlock-breaker made literal.
  • The make-good is the safety net. Copy states plainly: if Wiom under-delivers on leads, the ₹5,000 floor protects the CSP — this is the Income-Guarantee leg fused into the Compact.
  • Terms in plain Hindi, not legalese: ≥3 leads needed, 70% gate, paid ~14 days after month-end. One tap to accept.
  • On confirm: a success toast, the home MBG card appears, and the info banner retires.
Why a compact, not just a payout: Wiom's own ₹0→₹300 history proved per-install rate isn't the binding constraint — volume predictability is. So the headline commitment the CSP sees is Wiom's lead promise, not the rupee number. That is the lever the Cold Start decision is built on.

Stage 3 · Activated

MBG goes live on the home screen

The moment after opt-in: a confirmation, and a permanent home for the scheme so the CSP always knows where to look.

Trigger: opt-in confirmed Success toast + new MBG home card + assurance chip Tone: warm welcome
  • Success toast (WiomToast, Success) confirms enrolment without blocking the screen.
  • A new MBG progress card is inserted at the top of the home feed — the scheme's permanent home. On day 1 it shows the month's starting state and the days-left counter, so the clock is visible immediately.
  • A compact "गारंटी" assurance chip joins the 2×2 strip, mirroring the existing earnings/quality chips, so MBG lives alongside metrics the CSP already checks daily.
Why a persistent card, not just a one-time popup: the Compact is a 3-month relationship, not an announcement. A standing surface that updates daily is what turns "I signed up for something" into "I can see my guarantee filling up."

Stage 4 · Day-to-day eligibility

The guarantee updates live

This is the in-app version of the MBG simulator. The CSP can see, any day, exactly where they stand against the 70% gate and what they're projected to earn.

Trigger: each lead assigned / install completed MBG card (live) + tap → MBG drilldown screen Tone: factual, encouraging
  • The card's gauge is the simulator's gate-track: fill = current install rate, fixed mark at 70%. Green once cleared, amber while below.
  • Three live stats mirror the locked spec exactly — leads assigned (assignment-date cohort = denominator), installs (numerator), days left in the calendar month.
  • Projected payout uses the locked formula max(₹300×installs, ₹5,000) once the gate is cleared, shown as "अनुमानित" (estimated) so it never reads as a promise mid-month.
  • Tapping opens a full MBG drilldown (new screen in the home graph, alongside the existing earnings drilldown): per-lead status, the 14-day maturity clock on each open lead, and a plain-language "how it's calculated".
Why expose the mechanism: volume predictability is the product. A CSP who can watch the gauge fill trusts the guarantee; a black-box payout rebuilds the very uncertainty MBG exists to remove.

Stage 6 · At-risk

Near the cliff — the "one more install" nudge

The single highest-leverage moment in the whole journey. A CSP in the 60–69% band earns ₹0 make-good for almost the same work as one at 70%. The app's job: turn that cliff into a clear, cheap, achievable ask — before the month ends.

Trigger: rate in 60–69% OR slipping with open leads in hand Warning banner + chip warning + targeted push Tone: encouraging, specific, never scolding
  • Quantified nudge, not a vague warning. The app computes ceil(0.70 × leads) − installs and tells the CSP the exact number of installs to cross the gate and the rupee value it unlocks — the simulator's nudge logic, surfaced live.
  • Caution banner (WARNING severity, amber) on home; the assurance chip flips to "जोखिम में" with the warning style already supported (bonusAtRisk).
  • A targeted push fires when actionable leads are still in hand and time remains — the cheapest density Wiom can buy, aimed only at the near-miss cohort.
  • Tapping the banner opens a nudge sheet showing which open leads to close first (with their maturity clocks), so the ask is concrete.
Why show the cliff openly: hiding the 70% gate would make a later miss feel arbitrary and explosive. Naming it now — "बस 1 और इंस्टॉल" — converts a comms landmine into the most motivating message in the app.

Stage 7 · Parameter-specific failure handling

Which parameter is failing — and whose fault it is

"At-risk" is not one thing. MBG has three independent gates, each with a different cause, a different owner, and therefore a different message. The app must name the specific failing parameter — never a generic "you're failing."

Each maps to a distinct banner + drilldown reason Tone: no-blame, owner-aware

< 3 leads		 MBG marked "लागू नहीं" (not applicable) — not a failure. Install pay still flows. अभी आपको 3 से कम लीड मिली हैं, इसलिए इस महीने गारंटी लागू नहीं। यह आप पर निर्भर नहीं — हम आपके इलाके में और लीड भेजने पर काम कर रहे हैं। Wiom owns this, not the CSP. Low volume is a demand-side shortfall (the parallel intervention). Blaming the CSP for leads Wiom didn't send would break trust and the no-blame principle. The cliff, handled: Case C earns ~36% of Case B's payout for 60% of the work — correct by design, explosive in a wallet. The mitigation is sequencing: the Stage-6 nudge fires before the cliff so a miss is rarely a surprise, and this dialog never lets the miss read as punishment. "हम आपके साथ हैं" (we're with you) closes the loop on the Wiom way.
Stage 9 · Settlement

The 14-day maturity tail

A cohort doesn't fully resolve until ~14 days after month-end (the maturity window). The app must set this expectation so "where's my money?" never becomes a support ticket.

Trigger: month ends, cohort still maturing Info banner + wallet "pending" row Tone: clear, reassuring
  • An info banner states plainly that the guarantee amount is being calculated and will land within 14 days — the same wording used at opt-in, so there's no surprise.
  • The wallet shows a pending row for the MBG top-up (reusing the existing ledger pattern), with the expected credit date — so the CSP sees it's accounted for, not forgotten.
  • On credit, a success toast + the celebration dialog (Stage 8) fire together.
Why pre-empt this: the maturity tail is an open settlement item in the MBG spec. A silent 14-day gap between "month ended" and "money arrived" is exactly where trust erodes. Naming the date converts a worry into a countdown.

Stage 10 · Sunset & hand-off

30 September — the bridge closes, the ladder opens

The most strategically important screen. A clean, pre-announced sunset is what separates MBG from the Ola-2017 trap. The CSP is handed to the Earned-Flow Ladder — the persistence rail that was running in parallel all along.

Trigger: approaching / reaching 30 Sep Countdown banner → sunset sheet → Ladder intro Tone: graduation, not loss
  • Pre-announced, not abrupt. From mid-September a banner counts the scheme down — the temporariness promised at opt-in is now visibly kept.
  • The sunset sheet reframes the end as a graduation: the CSP has built capacity; now the Earned-Flow Ladder rewards it — better work earns more lead priority. No removal cliff, because the ladder was always there.
  • The MBG card is replaced (not deleted) by the Ladder status card, so the home screen never loses its earnings surface.
Why this matters most: the Cold Start floor is persistence — MBG only counts as success if behaviour survives its removal. Framing the sunset as "you've earned your place on the ladder" is the in-app moment that makes the igniter hand off to the sustainer instead of ending in a Rapido-style backlash.
